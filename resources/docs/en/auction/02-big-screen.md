# The big-screen view

The **Big screen** is what your audience watches — the TV at the venue, the projector on the wall, the LED billboard at the back of the room. Everyone in the room is looking at this; only the auctioneer is touching the laptop.

## Open it

In the left sidebar, click **Big screen**. The page opens in cinematic fullscreen mode — huge fonts, dark background, dramatic colors.

For the actual auction night:

- Connect a TV or projector to your laptop
- Open the Big screen page on that screen
- Press **F11** (Windows) or **Ctrl+Cmd+F** (Mac) to go fullscreen — this hides the browser address bar so it looks clean
- Walk away from it — the screen updates automatically as the auctioneer drives the auction

## What's on screen

- **Top header** — your tournament name, season, and a status pill (RUNNING, PAUSED, or WAITING) with a pulsing dot
- **Left panel** — the current player: large photo, name, position, base price, batting/bowling style, jersey number
- **Center top** — the **CURRENT BID** in giant gradient text, with the leading team's name underneath
- **Center bottom** — the **TIMER** in MM : SS format, also huge so the back row can see it
- **Right panel** — the **BID HISTORY** (the last 8 bids, with the latest highlighted)
- **Bottom strip** — every team's remaining budget with progress bars

## SOLD and UNSOLD animations

When a player is finalized:

- **Sold** — a giant stamp animation slams onto the screen showing the price and "Player Name to Team Name". It holds for 5 seconds and then fades out.
- **Unsold** — a permanent ink stamp design (like a real "UNSOLD" rubber stamp) lands on the screen with today's date. It stays until the next player loads.

These animations are designed to give the audience a clear visual moment — like a real-life auction hammer drop.

## Tips for the venue

- **Use a wired internet connection** if you can. Wi-Fi at venues can be unstable, and even small drops cause the bid display to lag.
- **Test from the back of the room.** The fonts auto-scale to your screen size, but venue lighting matters too. If it's hard to read from the back, dim the venue lights.
- **Hide the browser bar.** Press F11 (Windows) or Ctrl+Cmd+F (Mac) to make the browser go fullscreen.
- **Disable screensaver and sleep mode** on the venue computer so it doesn't black out mid-auction.

## Multiple big screens? No problem

You can open the big-screen page on as many devices as you like — they all show the same live auction. We've seen tournaments run with 6 screens at once (main hall, second hall, lobby, side rooms) without any issues.
