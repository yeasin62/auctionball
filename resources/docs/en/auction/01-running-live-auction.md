# Running a live auction

This is the main event. The auctioneer drives the auction from a laptop, the team owners bid from their phones, and the audience watches a TV or projector. Everything stays in sync automatically.

## Before you start — quick checklist

Run through this 30 minutes before your auction begins:

- [ ] Your tournament has an active season
- [ ] All teams are added with their budgets
- [ ] All players are added (and the ones you want to auction are in **queue** status)
- [ ] The auctioneer's laptop is on the same Wi-Fi as the team owners' phones
- [ ] The big-screen TV / projector is showing the **Big screen** page in fullscreen mode
- [ ] Each team owner either has the bidding link OR is logged in to their team account on their phone

If any of those isn't ready, fix it now — it'll save you scrambling later.

## The control panel

Click **Live auction** in the sidebar. You'll see three columns:

**Left column — Player queue.**
Every player in your active season, filterable by status (queue, sold, unsold). Click a player to load them as the **current player** — the one teams are about to bid on.

**Middle column — Current player.**
The player's photo, name, position, base price, and other details are shown big at the top. Below: the current bid amount, the timer, the action buttons (Start, Pause, Sold, Unsold, Reset), and a list of recent bids.

**Right column — Team budgets.**
Every team's remaining money, with a bar showing how much they've spent. Click a team to expand quick-bid buttons — useful when someone in the room shouts a bid and you want to record it for them.

## Running a single lot — step by step

1. **Pick a player** from the left queue. Their card loads in the middle.
2. **Pick a duration** — 30, 60, 90, or 120 seconds — from the **Next Player** dropdown
3. **Click Start** — the timer counts down, the bidding window is open
4. As bids come in from the team phones, the **Current bid** number updates in real time and the leading team's name appears
5. If a bid lands in the **last 10 seconds**, the timer automatically extends to 10 seconds. This stops last-second sniping — everyone gets a fair counter-bid window
6. If you need to give the room more time, click **+10s**, **+30s**, or **+60s** — or type any number of seconds in the **custom** box
7. When the timer hits zero:
   - With **auto-finalize on** (default): the player is automatically marked **sold** to the leading team, or **unsold** if no one bid
   - With **auto-finalize off**: the lot stays open until you click **Sold** or **Unsold** yourself

That's one lot. Then click the next player and repeat.

## Keyboard shortcuts (auctioneer's friend)

When you're not typing in a text box, these shortcuts work:

| Key | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Space** | Start, pause, or resume the timer |
| **S** | Sold |
| **U** | Unsold |
| **R** | Reset (clear bids, return to queue) |

The keyboard is faster than the mouse during a busy auction.

## What everyone else sees

- **Big screen** (open at `auctionball.com/dashboard/bigscreen`): a cinematic view with huge fonts — perfect for projecting onto the venue wall. Player photo, current bid, timer, leading team, recent bids, all team budgets at the bottom.
- **Rosters page**: every team's roster with the players they've bought. Auto-refreshes when new players are sold. Great to keep open on a second TV for the audience to track team rosters.

## "I made a mistake" — undoing things

> **I sold the wrong player / to the wrong team.**
> Find the player in the left queue. Click **Reset** in the action buttons. The bids are cleared, the team's budget is restored, and the player goes back to the queue. Then re-auction them.

> **I clicked Pause but actually want to resume.**
> Click **Resume** — the timer picks up where it left off, with the same number of seconds remaining.

> **I want to skip a player and come back later.**
> Click any other player in the queue. The current one stays in **idle** status with no bids on them. You can come back and click them again any time.
